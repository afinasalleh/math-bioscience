Optimizing Malaysian Poultry Production via Calculus

Using derivatives $f'(x)$ to determine peak daily weight gain in Broiler Chickens (Ross 308).

🐔 The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysian poultry management, feed costs constitute up to 70% of total production costs. To maximize Feed Conversion Efficiency (FCE), farm managers must know exactly when a broiler's growth rate peaks and when it begins to plateau.

Harvesting too early means missing out on rapid growth phase potential; harvesting too late means wasting expensive feed on an animal whose growth rate is slowing down.

Data Source Reference: Baseline parameters adapted from the Ross 308 Broiler Performance Objectives (Aviation/Aviagen) and localized Malaysian climate trials (MARDI).

🧮 The Mathematical Model

Animal growth typically follows a sigmoidal (S-shaped) Gompertz Function.

Growth Function $f(x)$
f(x) = W_max * e^(-b * e^(-k * x))

Yields cumulative weight (grams) at day $x$.

The Derivative $f'(x)$ (Rate of Change)
f'(x) = f(x) * b * k * e^(-k * x)

Calculates the precise growth velocity (grams/day) at day $x$.

Constants Used (Ross 308 standard):
• $W_{max}$ (Asymptotic weight) = 3500g
• $b$ (Biological constant) = 4.45
• $k$ (Growth rate modifier) = 0.072
